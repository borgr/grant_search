# Grant Discovery Skill

## Purpose
Systematically search for AI, tech, and research grants across multiple categories and discovery channels. Focus only on finding and documenting grants—not evaluation, outreach, or application strategy.

## Search Categories

### 1. Government & Institutional Funding
**Objective**: Federal, state, and international government grant opportunities.

**Search targets**:
- **US Federal**: Grants.gov (filter: AI, research, emerging tech), NSF (AI research categories), NIH (biomedical AI), DOE (scientific computing), DARPA (all solicitations), NIST (AI standards/tools)
- **State/Regional**: State science/tech grant programs, regional innovation centers
- **International**: EU Horizon Europe (HORIZON-CL4 digital/AI), Canada NSERC, UK Research Councils, Australian ARC, Singapore NRF, China NSFC
- **Bilateral/cross-border programs**: US-EU partnerships, US-Asia initiatives, Erasmus+ programs, Newton Fund

**Search queries**:
- "AI research grant [country/region]"
- "artificial intelligence funding [agency]"
- "[Agency] solicitation 2026"
- "international research collaboration grant"
- "bilateral research funding [countries]"

**Document**: Grant name, agency, funding amount, deadline, eligibility, URL

---

### 2. Private/Corporate Funding
**Objective**: Corporate grants, founder programs, and venture ecosystem funding.

**Search targets**:
- **Tech company grant arms**: Google.org, Microsoft Research, Amazon Science, Meta AI, OpenAI
- **Founder/billionaire initiatives**: Reid Hoffman Foundation, Stripe Grants, Bezos Day One Fund
- **Corporate ventures**: Google Ventures, Microsoft Ventures, Intel Capital
- **Industry consortia/foundations**: Partnership on AI, Linux Foundation, CNCF, IEEE, ACM

**Search queries**:
- "[Company] research grant AI"
- "[Founder] grant program"
- "corporate AI research sponsorship"

**Document**: Organization, program name, funding amount, focus areas, deadline, eligibility, URL

---

### 3. Philanthropic & Mission-Driven Funding
**Objective**: Philanthropic foundations focused on AI safety, research, and long-term impact.

**Search targets**:
- **AI Safety/Alignment**: Open Philanthropy, Survival & Flourishing Fund, Long-Term Future Fund, Future of Life Institute
- **Domain-specific foundations**: Ash Center (governance AI), Mozilla Foundation (responsible tech), Santa Fe Institute (complex systems), accessibility foundations
- **Regrants & platforms**: Manifund, Nonlinear Network
- **Independent researcher support**: Programs supporting researchers, alignment developers, AI safety engineers

**Key characteristics**:
- Open Philanthropy: ~$257K median, ~$63.6M (2024)
- Survival & Flourishing: ~$100K median, ~$35M (2025)
- Long-Term Future Fund: ~$25K median, rapid turnaround
- Manifund/Nonlinear: rolling regrants with low friction

**Search queries**:
- "AI safety grant"
- "existential risk funding"
- "longtermism AI research"
- "alignment researcher funding"
- "[Domain] foundation grant"

**Document**: Organization, focus area, typical grant size, methodology, notable grantees, deadline, URL

---

### 4. Non-Traditional Funding
**Objective**: Alternatives to traditional grants (prizes, residencies, accelerators, sponsorships).

**Search targets**:
- **Prize competitions**: Kaggle, X Prize, NeurIPS/ICML competitions, research competitions
- **Residency & fellowship programs**: Mozilla Fellowship, AI policy residencies, academic residencies
- **Accelerators/Incubators**: Y Combinator, Plug and Play (AI), Anterra, 500 Global
- **Sponsored research agreements**: Industry funding research in exchange for co-authorship/results
- **Conference travel & dissemination**: NSF travel support, workshop funding, open access publication grants
- **Paid advisory/consulting**: Companies funding research time through consulting

**Search queries**:
- "AI prize competition 2026"
- "fellowship program [domain]"
- "research residency funding"
- "conference travel grant"
- "workshop organization funding"
- "sponsored research opportunities"

**Document**: Program name, funding model, typical amounts, eligibility, deadline, URL

### 5. GPU & Compute Infrastructure Funding
**Objective**: Grants and programs providing GPU access, compute credits, or infrastructure for AI research.

**Search targets**:
- **Cloud provider credits**: AWS Research Credits, Google Cloud Credits, Azure for Research, Oracle Grants
- **GPU providers**: NVIDIA GPU Grants, NVIDIA NVAITC, Lambda Labs
- **Academic compute**: NSF ACCESS (formerly XSEDE), HPC partnerships
- **AI model provider compute**: Anthropic credits, OpenAI Researcher Access, Together AI, Hugging Face
- **Specialized compute funds**: Mozilla compute grants, AI safety compute programs, open-source project support

**Key characteristics**:
- Structured as credits/access, not cash
- Different eligibility (often easier for individuals/small teams)
- Rolling applications, fast decision cycles

**Search queries**:
- "GPU grant researchers"
- "[Cloud provider] research credits"
- "free GPU access AI"
- "NVIDIA research GPU"
- "academic supercomputing access"

**Document**: Provider, program, compute type/volume, allocation mechanism, award size, deadline, eligibility, URL

**Search targets**:

**Search queries**:

- "BigScience collaborative funding"

### 9. Open Source & Community-Driven Research Funding
**Objective**: Open source projects, community science, and decentralized research collaboration.

**Search targets**:
- **Open source funding**: Open Technology Fund, Software Freedom Conservancy, open source AI projects

**Discovery platforms**: GitHub Sponsors, OpenCollective, ResearchHub, DAO platforms (Snapshot), Zooniverse, Experiment.com, community Discord/Slack

### 10. Discovery Channels & Aggregators
**Objective**: Use specialized tools and communities to surface ongoing opportunities across all categories.

**Traditional funding channels**:
- **Funding aggregators**: GrantHub, Instrumentl, Foundation Center, GrantStation
- **Government agency portals**: Grants.gov, agency-specific solicitation pages (DARPA, NSF, NIH, DOE)
- **Email/newsletters**: Funding alert services, university research notices, "AI grants digest"
- **Social media**: Follow program officers, researchers on Twitter/LinkedIn (search: "grant opening", "RFP", "funding available")
- **Academic communities**: NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR funding pages, conference sponsorship opportunities
- **Mailing lists**: ResearchGate, Academia.edu announcements, arXiv funding sections
- **University research offices**: Contact grant offices for curated opportunity lists
- **Foundation databases**: Foundation Directory, 990 filings for comparable grants
- **Research communities**: AI safety Slack, Effective Altruism communities, alignment research networks

**Community & open source channels**:
- **GitHub Sponsors**: Active research project sponsor programs
- **OpenCollective**: Research collectives and funding campaigns
- **ResearchHub**: Community bounties and marketplace
- **DAO platforms**: Snapshot governance, community research DAOs
- **Community forums**: Discord/Slack (AI research, open science networks), Reddit (r/MachineLearning)
- **Science platforms**: Zooniverse (citizen science), Experiment.com (crowdfunded research)
- **Open science**: Open Science Foundation, PrePrints.org, OSF

**Search queries**:
- "AI funding opportunities 2026"
- "research grants [topic]" on LinkedIn/Twitter
- "research bounty [topic]" on ResearchHub
- "community science funding" on Zooniverse

**Document**: Source URL, access method, update frequency, coverage notes

## Output Format

Add all new grants found to a json and notify the user that new grants have been added.


Create entries in `potential_new_grants.json` with:
```json
{
  "name": "Grant/Program Name",
  "source_category": "1-10 (Government, Corporate, Philanthropic, Non-Traditional, GPU/Compute, Lab/Infrastructure, Career, Large-Scale, Open-Source, Discovery)",
  "grant_type": "Research | Career-Development | Travel-Dissemination | Infrastructure",
  "organization": "Granting organization",
  "funding_amount_min": 0,
  "funding_amount_max": 0,
  "deadline": "YYYY-MM-DD or 'Rolling' or 'TBD'",
  "focus_areas": ["AI", "Research", "..."],
  "eligibility": "Brief summary of who can apply",
  "url": "Direct link to program page",
  "discovered_date": "YYYY-MM-DD",
  "notes": "Any additional context"
}
```

**grant_type** field disambiguates use cases:
- **Research**: Primary research funding for projects/investigations
- **Career-Development**: Postdoc fellowships, early-career awards, career transition support
- **Travel-Dissemination**: Conference travel, workshop organization, publication/dissemination support
- **Infrastructure**: Lab equipment, facility development, instrumentation (non-compute)

---

## Search Execution

1. **Systematic approach**: Work through each category sequentially
2. **Parallel where possible**: Multiple searches simultaneously
3. **Document as you go**: Add to potential_new_grants.json as you discover (don't batch at end)
4. **Capture metadata**: Note search queries and sources for reproducibility
5. **Avoid duplicates**: Use `is_existing.py` to check each entry against found_grants.json before adding
   - Run: `python is_existing.py --check <entry_json>` for single entry or batch
   - Detects: exact matches, fuzzy name/org similarity, URL matches, same organization
   - Catches variations like "NSF AI Grants" vs "National Science Foundation - AI Research"

---

## Notes

- **Not included**: Evaluation of grants, strategic fit analysis, application preparation, outreach
- **Focus**: Breadth of discovery across all categories
- **Update frequency**: Re-run quarterly (funding landscape shifts)
- **Coverage check**: After searching, analyze found_grants.json for gaps by geography, stage, vertical
